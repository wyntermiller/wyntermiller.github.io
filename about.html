Wynter is an editor and writer based in San Francisco. She is currently the Managing Editor at Electric Literature, where she supports the publication of two literary magazines.

Wynter's editorial focus is literary fiction. Many of the short stories she has edited are now published in full-length collections, including “The Last Unmapped Places” by Rebecca Turkewitz, “The Oracle” by Joanna Pearson (Pushcart Prize Special Mention), and “Group Sex” by Elisa Faison. At Electric Lit, Wynter also acquires serial fiction. Her selections have garnered numerous accolades, including the O. Henry Prize (Winner; “Didi” by Amber Caron), the Story Prize (Finalist; “A Book of New Grotesques” by Jessi Jezewska Stevens), and inclusion in Best American Short Stories (Distinguished Story; “Chicken-Flavored, Lemon-Scented” by Katherine Heiny). During her career, Wynter has worked with distinguished writers such as Karen Russell, Weike Wang, Ramona Ausubel, Pam Houston, and Aimee Bender, among others.

Prior to her work in publishing, Wynter was a legal editor and scholar. After earning her JD in 2017, Wynter completed a postdoctoral fellowship at the National Institutes of Health in the Department of Bioethics, where she was a developmental editor. Her academic writing is published in premier journals, including the Annals of Internal Medicine, American Journal of Bioethics, Washington & Lee Law Review, Tennessee Law Review, and UC Davis Law Review.
